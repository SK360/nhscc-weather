NHSCC Weather & Attendance — 20 Years of Data

Over 20 seasons of NHSCC autocross at North Park, one thing stands out above everything else: rain kills attendance. This analysis matched historical weather records from the ERA5 climate dataset — the same data used by meteorologists — to every single NHSCC event date and looked for patterns. Precipitation is measured during event hours only (8 AM – 5 PM Eastern), so overnight rain doesn’t inflate the numbers. Here’s what the data says.

☔ Rain Is the Biggest Factor

The rain correlation coefficient is , which means there’s a clear and consistent negative relationship — more rain during the event reliably means fewer drivers. It’s the strongest weather signal in the data.

🌡 Temperature Matters, But Less Than You’d Think

Warmer days do tend to draw more drivers — the correlation is — but the sweet spot appears to be warm days with highs in the 68–77°F range, which averaged drivers. Hot days (78°F+) averaged a similar — warm weather in general is good for turnout.

Cold events are the real attendance killers on the temperature side. Events where the high didn’t crack 45°F averaged only drivers — the lowest of any temperature bucket.

☁ Clouds Don’t Scare Drivers

One of the more counterintuitive findings: overcast-but-dry days are nearly as good as sunny ones. Sunny and clear events averaged drivers. Overcast dry days averaged . This matters because Pittsburgh gets a lot of overcast days — and the data says they're not a problem.

📌 The Bottom Line

If you want to predict whether an NHSCC event will have strong turnout, check two things: Is it going to rain? And will the high temp be above 55°F? Get both of those right and you’re almost guaranteed a solid crowd.
